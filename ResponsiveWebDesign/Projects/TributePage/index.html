Ronald Reagan

Actor, Governor, President

Here are some highlights from Ronald Reagan's life:

  • 1911 - Born February 6 in Tampico, Illinois.
  • 1932 - Graduated from Eureka College in Illinois. Began radio career at WOC/Davenport, covering the University of Iowa's homecoming game against Minnesota.
  • 1937 - While in Southern California to cover the Chicago Cubs’ spring training season, Ronald Reagan did a screen test for the Warner Brothers movie studio. The studio signed him to a contract, and that same year he made his film debut in “Love is on the Air,” playing a radio news reporter.
  • 1939-1945 - Disqualified from combat duty due to poor eyesight. Spent his time in the Army making training films.
  • 1930s-1960s - Appeared in more than 50 movies.
  • 1947 to 1952, and from 1959 to 1960 - Served as president of the Screen Actors Guild (SAG).
  • 1966 - Defeated Democratic incumbent Edmund “Pat” Brown Sr to become governor of California.
  • 1970 - Reelected to second term of the governorship of California.
  • 1980 - Wins Republican party bid for President after two unsuccessful bids.
  • 1981 - Sworn in as President of the United States on January 20, when he famously said:

    • “In this present crisis, government is not the solution to our problems; government is the problem.”

  • 1981 - Survived assassination attempt by John Hinkley Jr. on March 30.
  • 1981 - Made history by appointing Sandra Day O’Connor (1930-) as the first woman to the U.S. Supreme Court.
  • 1983 - Announced the Strategic Defense Initiative (SDI), a plan to develop space-based weapons to protect America from attacks by Soviet nuclear missiles.
  • 1987 - Americans and Soviets signed a historic agreement to eliminate intermediate-range nuclear missiles. That same year, Reagan spoke at Germany’s Berlin Wall, a symbol of communism, and famously challenged Gorbachev to tear it down.
  • 1989 - After leaving the White House in January, Reagan and wife Nancy return to California.
  • 1991 - The Ronald Reagan Presidential Library and Museum opened in Simi Valley, California.
  • 1994 - Reagan revealed in November in a handwritten letter that he had recently been diasgnosed with Alzheimer's disease.
  • 2004 - On June 5, Reagan died at his home in Los Angeles.
